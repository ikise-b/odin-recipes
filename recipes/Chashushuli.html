Chashushuli

Ostri is another typical Georgian food - a spicy beef stew resembling an European goulash. The beef is cooked together with tomatoes and mushrooms and further flavored with generous amount of garlic, spices and herbs. It´s a hearty, filling dish ideal for chilly evenings.

On some restaurant menus, you will find also a similar dish named chashushuli. As a culinary layman, they both looked same to me. Only later, a friend explained me the difference. During the preparation of ostri, ingredients are cooked together from the beginning. But in case of chashushuli, the meat is first briefly fried and then cooked in its own oil - tomatoes and mushrooms are added only much later.

Ingredients

  • Beef (usually stewing cuts like chuck or brisket)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomatoes (or tomato paste)
  • Bell pepper (optional)
  • Carrot (optional)
  • Bay leaves
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro
  • Olive oil (or any cooking oil)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Water or broth

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Brown the beef pieces on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté chopped onions until softened.
  4. Add minced garlic and sauté for another minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes (or tomato paste) and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Return the browned beef to the pot.
  7. Add bay leaves, salt, and pepper. If using, pour in red wine.
  8. Cover with water or broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 1.5-2 hours until beef is tender.
  9. Optional: Add carrots or bell pepper during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  10. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro before serving.
