Churchkhela

Churchkhela is a traditional Georgian sweet with a shape resembling that of an overgrown pea pod. It's made of nuts put on a string and repeatedly dipped in a mix of grape juice, flour, and honey. The most common are churchkhelas made of walnuts but sometimes are used also hazelnuts, almonds or para nuts. Churchkhela doesn´t have a too striking taste but since it´s light, durable and nutritious, it´s worth it to take a few pieces to the mountain treks - it´s no coincidence that it´s called "Georgian Snickers". After all, it was a favorite meal of Georgian warriors riding into battles. And it's also a very popular souvenir.

Ingredients

  • Grape juice (or wine, traditionally)
  • Walnuts (whole or halves)
  • Sugar
  • Flour (optional, for thickening)
  • Cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • Honey (optional, for sweetness)
  • Salt (optional, for flavor balance)

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring the grape juice (or wine) to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer.
  2. Mix in sugar, stirring until completely dissolved. You can also add honey or salt at this stage for flavor adjustments.
  3. If you want a thicker coating, mix some flour or cornstarch with a bit of cold water, then slowly add it to the simmering juice while stirring to avoid lumps.
  4. Continue to cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens to a syrupy consistency (about 15-20 minutes).
  5. While the mixture thickens, prepare your walnuts. Thread them onto strings, tying knots between each nut to create a chain (this is the base for your Churchkhela). You can also alternate with other dried fruits, if desired.
  6. Once the syrup is thick enough, dip each walnut chain into the syrup, making sure to coat each piece thoroughly.
  7. Hang the dipped walnut strings to dry in a cool, dry place for several hours or overnight. You can hang them on a clothesline or a rod to let them drip and set.
  8. After they've dried and firmed up, they'll be ready to eat! Churchkhela can be kept for weeks, stored in a cool, dry place.
